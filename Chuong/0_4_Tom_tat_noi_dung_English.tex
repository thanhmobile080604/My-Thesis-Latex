\begin{center}
    \Large{\textbf{ABSTRACT}}\\
\end{center}
\vspace{1cm}

In the context of modern mobile devices increasingly equipped with various sensors such as satellite positioning, inertial sensors, and cameras, the problem of real-time movement tracking has gained new approaches. However, in real-world environments such as dense urban areas, locations with many obstructions, or unstable device conditions, satellite positioning signals may degrade, lose updates, or produce inaccurate results. This directly affects the ability to determine the user’s location and display their route on the map.

This thesis adopts the approach of developing a mobile application on the Android platform to investigate the ability to combine camera data and mobile sensors to support location updates when satellite positioning signals are unreliable. The system is organized into three main functional flows: satellite information viewing, optical flow viewing through the camera, and live navigation supported by optical flow.

The proposed solution forms a visual testing and observation system, ranging from collecting the user’s location, satellite status, and raw positioning measurements to visualizing the results on a two-dimensional map, a three-dimensional Earth model, and an augmented reality view. In addition, the system uses OpenCV to analyze real-time camera streams, supports region-of-interest selection and object tracking, integrates a deep learning model server to analyze optical flow in uploaded videos, stores measurement sessions, and evaluates processing metrics.

The main contribution of this thesis is the development of an Android application that supports the investigation of combining satellite positioning, inertial sensors, and optical flow in the live navigation problem. When satellite positioning signals degrade, the application uses data from the camera and inertial sensors to estimate speed and movement direction, while assisting position correction along the current route. This provides a practical basis for evaluating positioning support in situations where satellite signals are unstable.
